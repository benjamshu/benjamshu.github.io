Now, arielle, i know it's your first time working like this, but kalle and ella are a very nice couple, and i'll be there with you every step of the way, 'k?

'k… Arielle looked, and sounded, rather uncertain.

Mina gave her a playful shove. Oh, come on, she said. It's not like you'll be doing anything you didn't do at the orphanage; just the place has changed, that's all. Isn't it nice to get out of that musty old building, anyways?

I guess so, arielle said, shrugging. She looked at her feet as she walked; she didn't look convinced.

Mina sighed. Chin up, ari, it's not like you to be so down.

Arielle looked up at her and pouted. But i don't want to clean people's houses!

Mina smiled and tousled her hair. Now that's the ari i know. C'mon, it won't be that bad, and if we don't work then how will margaret afford to feed us? Or what about myrra, you gonna make her work instead?

No… arielle grumbled.

You're a big sister now, mina said, punching her on the shoulder. That means you get to help take care of the younger ones. Now enough fighting; we're here.

They walked up the steps and knocked on the door. It was opened by an elderly woman, who greeted them with a smile. Hello, girls, she said. There are two of you this time!

Mina bowed graciously, nudging arielle, who followed suit. Morning, ma'am, she said. It's arielle's first time; i'm just here to help out.

The woman nodded her head. Mm, well, i seem to remember you doing a good job, she said, stepping aside to let them in. Kalle is inside; come in, i'll show you what needs to be done.

There wasn't much. Kalle and ella were fairly well-off, and had a cook and general housemaid on staff. They only called in extra help to get the place looking nice before hosting a function—dusting the furniture, polishing the silver, that sort of thing—and it was really only a one-person job at best. Consequently, the two finished with plenty of time to spare. Better run home, then, ella said, handing arielle their payment. I'm sure we can manage here.

They stepped out onto the street and began the walk back. Arielle looked upwards at mina. I liked ella, she said.

Mina gave her a nudge. Told you, she said. The sun hung low in the sky behind them, and long shadows fell before the duo. It was autumn, and the air was brisk but not yet cold. An elderly fellow in a suit approached them, his cane swinging as he strode. The girls nodded respectfully as he passed.
